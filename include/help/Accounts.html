Overview

Use the Accounts module to record a client’s investment accounts, liabilities, and other assets. All users can view Accounts.

Availability

The Accounts module is available to the following editions:

  • Standard
  • Professional
  • Enterprise

Actions

Create an Account

Create an Account by completing and saving the Create Accounts form.

  1. Access the form, using one of the following paths:
    • From the Navigation menu, click Accounts > Accounts > Create Accounts.
    • From the Navigation menu, click Accounts > Accounts. In the Accounts List View, click Create New.
    • While viewing a Create Account form, click Create New.

    The Create Account form displays.

  2. Complete the form. Descriptions for each field are included below.
  3. Click Save.

    The account is included in the Account List View.

Overview dashlet field descriptions

In the Overview dashlet, enter the information for the following fields. Fields that have an asterisk (*) are required; you must enter data in the field to save the account.

Account Name:*

Enter a descriptive name to use when referencing the account.

Account Type:

Click the category to which this account belongs on the Account Type Popup List View (displays upon clicking the Selection arrow) or the list of categories matching the character(s) you enter.

Used in reporting, this field is required if the account is to appear in net worth statements. To add or edit account types see Account Types.

Account Number:

Enter the unique account number or certificate number assigned by the financial institution including all punctuation such as hyphens. The Account Number will be used to match account-related imports so it must match any external systems in use by the firm.

Custodian:

Click the financial institution that holds the asset or liability from the Companies Popup List View (displays upon clicking the Selection arrow) or the list of companies matching the character(s) you enter.

Value:

Enter the most current value of the asset or balance of the account.

Description:

Enter additional details about the account.

Client:

Click the client associated with the account from the Client Popup List View (displays upon clicking the Selection arrow) or the list of clients matching the character(s) you enter.

Ownership Type:*

From the dropdown, click the item describing how the account is owned. This field is required if the Account is to appear in net worth statements.

Owner:

If the account is owned by a single individual, click the owner from the Contacts Popup List View (displays upon clicking the Selection arrow) or the list of owners matching the character(s) you enter.

Managed:

Click Yes if managed by the firm; otherwise, click No.

Value Date:

Click in the Date Selector (or enter the date) on which the account value was updated.

Teams:

By default, your private team is assigned as the primary team.

To change the primary team assignment, click the team on the Teams Popup List View (displays upon clicking the Selection arrow) or the list of teams matching the character(s) you enter.

To assign additional teams to the Account, click add ( + ) and click the team as described above.

For more information on assigning teams, see Assigning records to Users and Teams.

Settings dashlet field descriptions

In the Settings dashlet, check the boxes associated with the following fields if they apply to this account.

AUM:
(Assets Under Management)

The account is managed by the firm.

Discretionary:

The client has given the firm trading authority for this account.

LPOA:
(Limited Power of Attorney)

The client has signed a limited power of attorney form, often included as part of a discount brokerage application.

Include on Account Policy:

The account should be included on the client’s Investment Policy Statement.

EFT:
(Electronic Funds Transfer)

This account has electronic funds transfer capability.

Qualified Plan:

The account is an employer-sponsored retirement plan that qualifies for special tax treatment.

RMD:
(Required Minimum Distribution)

The account is subject to Required Minimum Distributions. This applies to tax-deferred or tax-free accounts where the account owner is at least 70.5 years old OR where the account owner inherited the account from someone who was subject to RMD.

Withdrawal Rate Asset:

The account is to be included when calculating the client’s rate of withdrawals for normal living expenses.

FPOA (Custody):
(Full Power of Attorney)

The client has signed a full power of attorney form. This means that the firm has custody of client funds, usually in a trustee capacity.

Margin Account:

This is a brokerage account in which the broker lends cash to purchase securities.

Checkwriting:

The client has the ability to write checks against this account.

Policy dashlet field descriptions

In the Policy dashlet, enter the information for the following fields or check the box if it applies to the account.

Investment Policy:

Enter a description of the overall investment policy for this account.

Target Return:

Enter the expected rate of return for this account.

Risk Tolerance:

Click the level of risk the client finds acceptable.

Allocation Stocks:

Enter a range for the acceptable percentage of the account invested in stocks.

Allocation Bonds:

Enter a range for the acceptable percentage of the account invested in bonds.

Allocation Cash:

Enter a range for the acceptable percentage of the account invested in cash.

Reinvest Interest:

Check to reinvest interest income.

Reinvest Dividends:

Check to reinvest equity dividends generated by the account.

Policy Date:

Click in the Date Selector (or enter) the date on which the policy became effective.

Investment Objective:

Click the client’s investment goal for this account from the drop-down list.

Withdrawal Requirement:

Click the age at which withdrawals must begin, or the client’s liquidity needs.

Target Stocks:

Enter the desired percentage of the account invested in stocks.

Target Bonds:

Enter the desired percentage of the account invested in bonds.

Target Cash:

Enter the desired percentage of the account invested in cash.

Reinvest Capital Gains:

Check to reinvest profits from account capital gains.

Cash dashlet field descriptions

In the Cash dashlet, enter the information for the following fields.

Cash Balance:

Enter the amount of cash in the account.

Cash Balance High $:

Enter the largest amount of cash acceptable in this account.

Cash Balance Low $:

Enter the minimum amount of cash acceptable in this account.

Cash Balance Date:

Click in the Date Selector (or enter) the date on which the cash balance was updated.

Cash Balance High %:

Enter the largest amount of cash acceptable in this account as a percentage of the current account value.

Cash Balance Low %:

Enter the minimum amount of cash acceptable in this account as a percentage of the current account value.

Account Mgmt dashlet field descriptions

In the Account Mgmt dashlet, enter the information for the following fields.

Account Website:

Enter the URL used for online account management.

Username:

Enter the username used to manage the account.

Password:

Enter the password to manage the account.

PIN:
(Personal Identification Number)

Enter a PIN or secondary password for sites with multiple authentication factors.

Routing Number:

Enter the routing transit number for bank accounts.

Master Account:

Enter the master account number assigned by the financial institution to aggregate client accounts.

Master account numbers are assigned by both Fidelity and Charles Schwab. It is a way for these custodians to group all advisor accounts together. When a custodian sends us a file it may contain multiple accounts. Some advisers have both discretionary and non-discretionary accounts. The custodian will assign separate master account numbers for each. That is to say, there will be a master account number for all discretionary accounts, and another master account for non-discretionary accounts.

Rep Name:

Enter the name of the person at the financial institution to contact regarding this account.

Rep Phone:

Enter the account representative’s phone number.

Rep Email:

Enter the account representative’s email address.

Rep Fax:

Enter the account representative’s fax number.

Billing dashlet field descriptions

In the Billing dashlet, enter the information for the following fields, or check boxes if they apply to the account.

Billed Account:

Check the box if the firm will debit fees from this account.

Billing Policy:

Enter a description of the policy applied to this account for billing.

Billing Exceptions:

Enter any exceptions to the billing policy such as excluded positions.

Billed Amount:

If the account is billed at a flat rate, enter the amount to be billed.

Billing Policy Date:

Click on the Date Selector (or enter) the date the billing policy was last updated.

Billed Percent:

If the account is billed on a percentage basis, enter the percent value.

Loan dashlet field descriptions

In the Loan dashlet, enter the information for the following fields.

Initial Amount:

Enter the original amount of the account. In the case of a loan, this is the amount borrowed.

Maturity Date:

Click on the Date Selector (or enter) the date the account comes due; e.g., if the account is a loan or CD.

Payment Frequency:

If the account is a liability, click how often payments are made.

Payment:

Enter the monthly payment amount, total principal plus interest.

APR:
(Annual Percentage Rate)

If the account is a loan, enter the annual percentage rate.

Term:

Enter duration of the loan in months.

PMI:
(Private Mortgage Insurance)

If the account is a mortgage, enter the private mortgage insurance amount.

Misc dashlet field descriptions

In the Misc dashlet, enter the information for the following fields.

APY:
(Annual Percentage Yield)

If the account earns interest, enter the annual percentage yield.

Commission Schedule:

If commissions apply to the sale of this asset, select the commission schedule.

History dashlet field descriptions

In the History dashlet, enter the information for the following fields and check boxes that apply to the account.

Date Opened:

Click on the Date Selector (or enter) the date the account was opened.

Source of Funds:

Enter the source of funds for an investment such as inheritance, sale of property, or employment income.

Cost Basis Date:

Click on the Date Selector (or enter) the date of the cost basis.

Original Owner:

Enter the full name of the original account owner. This is especially important for inherited RMD accounts.

Original Owner Date of Birth:

Click on the Date Selector (or enter) the date of birth of the original account owner.

Closed:

Check the box if the account has been closed but is being saved for reference.

When an account is closed, update the Value in the Overview dashlet to zero (0) and set the Value Date (in the Overview dashlet) to the date the account was closed.

Closed By User:

Click the person who closed the account on the User Popup List View (displays upon clicking the Selection arrow) or the list of users matching the character(s) you enter.

Cost Basis:

Enter the original value or purchase price of an asset.

The purpose of the cost basis field is to hold a static cost basis for something that rarely changes in value; e.g., a piece of land, or for an apartment building. Cost basis for securities is dynamic, and should be maintained in a portfolio management system.

Original Owner Date of Death:

Click on the Date Selector (or enter) the date of death of the original account owner.

Date Closed:

Click on the Date Selector (or enter) the date the account was closed.

Edit an Account

Edit the information for an account in the Accounts Edit View.

  1. Access the Accounts Edit View, using one of the following paths:
    • From the Navigation, click Accounts > Accounts > View Accounts.
    • From the Navigation, click Accounts > Accounts. In the Accounts list, click the edit icon.
    • While in the Accounts Detail View, click Edit.

    The Accounts form displays.
  2. Complete the form. Descriptions for each field are included above.
  3. Click Save.

Delete an Account

Delete an account from the Account list.

  1. Access the Accounts List View, using one of the following paths:
    • From the Navigation, click Accounts > Accounts > View Accounts.
    • From the Navigation, click Accounts > Accounts.

    The Accounts List View displays.
  2. Click the selection box of each account you want to delete.
  3. Click Delete.
